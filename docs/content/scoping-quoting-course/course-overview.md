## Course Description

Every profitable restoration job starts with an accurate scope. Yet scoping remains one of the most under-taught skills in the Australian restoration industry — too often learned through trial, error and expensive rework.

**Scoping & Quoting for Restoration Professionals** is a comprehensive 8-module course designed for restoration technicians, estimators and project managers who want to master the commercial and technical skills required to scope, document and price water, fire and mould restoration jobs with confidence.

Starting with the fundamentals — what a scope of works actually is, how it differs from a quote, and the industry standards that govern it — the course builds systematically through site assessment, moisture mapping, and evidence documentation. You will learn the IICRC S500 classification system (categories and classes of water damage) and apply it directly to real-world scoping decisions. Modules on mould remediation scoping (per IICRC S520) and fire/smoke damage scoping expand your capability across the full range of restoration losses.

The pricing and estimating module takes the mystery out of profitability. You will learn to use industry software (Xactimate and SimPRO), calculate fully loaded labour rates, compare equipment hire versus ownership costs, and set pricing that achieves your target margin — not just your target markup. The distinction between the two is one of the most misunderstood concepts in the trade, and this course makes it crystal clear.

Working with insurers is a reality of Australian restoration. The insurance and client communication module prepares you to present scopes to loss adjusters, write supplements that get approved, and manage client expectations throughout the project lifecycle. You will understand the claims process from the adjuster's perspective, giving you a strategic advantage in every negotiation.

The course culminates in two capstone case studies — a residential water loss and a commercial fire damage scenario — where you produce complete scope packages from raw inspection data through to priced estimates. These are the closest thing to on-the-job experience without leaving your desk.

All content aligns with IICRC S500, S520 and S540 standards, Australian/New Zealand standards (AS/NZS 4349.1), and WHS legislation. Whether you are a technician stepping into an estimating role, an experienced estimator looking to sharpen your process, or a business owner who wants tighter scopes and fatter margins, this course delivers practical, immediately applicable skills.

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of the restoration process (recommended: IICRC WRT certification or equivalent field experience)
- Familiarity with common restoration equipment (dehumidifiers, air movers, moisture meters)
- No prior estimating software experience required — the course covers Xactimate and SimPRO from the ground up

---

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Conduct a systematic site inspection** using a structured protocol that captures all relevant data for scoping, including WHS hazard identification
2. **Classify water damage** by IICRC S500 category (contamination) and class (absorption extent) and apply the correct scoping approach for each combination
3. **Produce professional moisture maps** using pin and pinless moisture meters, establishing dry standards and defining damage boundaries
4. **Write complete scopes of works** for water, mould and fire/smoke damage restoration projects with defensible line items cross-referenced to evidence
5. **Price restoration jobs profitably** using Xactimate and SimPRO, calculating fully loaded labour rates, equipment costs and target gross margins
6. **Present scopes to loss adjusters** and write supplements that are supported by evidence, standards references and clear justification
7. **Manage client communication** throughout the restoration lifecycle, setting realistic expectations and resolving disputes professionally
8. **Produce a complete scope package** (inspection report, moisture map, scope of works, priced estimate) for residential and commercial losses

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Who is this course for?

This course is for anyone in the restoration industry who writes or reviews scopes of works — technicians moving into estimating, current estimators, project managers and business owners. It is also valuable for insurance building consultants who assess restoration scopes.

### 2. Do I need IICRC WRT certification before enrolling?

WRT certification is recommended but not required. You should have a basic understanding of the restoration process and common equipment. If you are completely new to the industry, we recommend completing an introductory restoration course first.

### 3. How long do I have to complete the course?

You have 12 months from the date of enrolment to complete all modules and the final assessment. Most students complete the course within 4–6 weeks at a pace of 2–3 hours per week.

### 4. What is the pass mark for the final assessment?

The module quizzes require 70% to pass. The final assessment (Module 8) requires 80%. You may reattempt assessments up to three times.

### 5. Will I learn Xactimate?

Yes. Module 6 includes hands-on instruction for navigating Xactimate with Australian price lists, building line-item estimates, and adjusting for local conditions. SimPRO is also covered for job management and private work quoting.

### 6. How do IICRC CECs work?

Upon completing the course and passing the final assessment, you receive 8 IICRC Continuing Education Credits (CECs). These credits are recorded against your IICRC certification profile and contribute to your ongoing certification maintenance requirements.

### 7. Is this course recognised by Australian insurers?

While no specific course is mandated by insurers, the content aligns with IICRC S500, S520 and S540 — the standards Australian insurers and loss adjusters reference when assessing restoration scopes. Completing this course demonstrates your commitment to industry best practice.

### 8. Can I access the course on my phone or tablet?

Yes. The CARSI LMS is fully responsive and available as a Progressive Web App (PWA). You can access lessons, take quizzes and review materials on any device with a modern browser. Offline access is available for the lesson you are currently viewing.
